Building the Diagnostic Before the First Test

Most brands want to start testing on day one. The problem is that day-one hypotheses are guesses — made without knowing where the funnel actually leaks, which users are converting, or what's stopping the ones who aren't. This engagement shows the full pre-test diagnostic framework and the three tests it produced.

Why the Diagnostic Comes First

A CRO program without a prior diagnostic is a random walk through the hypothesis space. You test things that look like good ideas — "add social proof," "simplify the checkout," "improve the hero copy" — and you get results that don't compound because each test was answering a different question about a different problem.

The diagnostic phase answers one question before any test is scoped: where is the revenue actually leaking, and why? Once that's established, the test roadmap isn't a list of ideas — it's an ordered queue of answers to a specific question.

The methodology shown here is the work. Cannabis Accessories Brand is a cannabis accessories brand on Shopify. The engagement began with a full pre-test audit — seven parallel workstreams, 50+ hours of diagnostic work — before a single test was proposed. The three tests that emerged from it had specific, data-backed rationale. None were guesses.

The Audit Framework — 7 Workstreams

01
Analytics Audit
7 hrs
GA4 setup accuracy, event tracking completeness, data cleanliness. Establish whether the data can be trusted before reading it.
02
Traffic Source Analysis
7 hrs
Which channels drive sessions vs conversions. Organic, paid, referral, social — and which ones are sending buyers vs browsers.
03
Funnel Analysis
6 hrs
Landing → product → cart → checkout → purchase. Where are the step-by-step drop-offs and what's their relative size?
04
Cart & Checkout Analysis
6 hrs
Abandonment rates at each checkout step. Payment-step exits. Friction points between add-to-cart and completed purchase.
05
Qualitative Analysis
18 hrs
Heuristic evaluation, session recordings, heatmap & scroll analysis, customer journey mapping. The "why" behind the quantitative numbers.
06
VoC: Surveys
15 hrs
On-site pre-purchase survey, post-purchase survey, exit-intent survey. What buyers say they needed and what stopped the ones who didn't convert.
07
Review Mining
20 hrs
Sentiment analysis, theme extraction, buying triggers, conversion barriers. Scaled VoC from existing customer language.

What the Diagnostic Produces

A structured pre-test audit doesn't just identify problems — it produces five distinct outputs that a test roadmap requires:

Problem Areas
Specific pages, funnels, or elements with statistically meaningful conversion suppression — not guesses.
Segmentation Insights
How mobile vs desktop, new vs returning, and traffic source segments perform differently — so tests aren't designed for the wrong user.
User Intent Map
Why users behave the way they do — why they abandon carts, why they don't add to cart, what information gap is blocking purchase.
Behavioral Signals
Friction points, confusing navigation, ignored sections, misread visual hierarchy — from recordings and heatmaps, not assumptions.
Data-Backed Hypotheses
Each hypothesis is traceable back to a specific data signal. The test answers a question the diagnostic identified, not a best-practice list.

The Three Tests That Emerged

Fifty hours of diagnostic work produced a prioritized initial test pipeline. Three tests were scoped for week one — each traceable to a specific finding from a specific workstream:

Site-wide Header · Desktop & Mobile
Sticky Header Search + Autosuggest
Exposed, helpful search increases search click-through rate, product discovery, and downstream Add-to-Cart. Source: Funnel analysis showed significant drop-off at the product discovery step — users arriving but not reaching product pages. Search is the fastest path to intent-matched products.
Implementation: ~45 hrs · Primary KPIs: PDP visits, ATC rate, CVR
Homepage — Above the Fold
Visual Trust Row
Clear first action with reassurance reduces bounce and increases category click-through. Source: Session recordings and heuristic evaluation showed homepage arrivals without a clear next step. Trust signals (guarantee, shipping, reviews) and a single clear CTA reduce the hesitation that causes immediate exits.
Implementation: ~25 hrs · Primary KPIs: PLP/PDP visit rate, ATC rate, CVR
Product Detail Page — Multi-variant Products
Variant Chips vs Dropdown
Faster variant selection increases Add-to-Cart rate. Source: Heatmap data showed interaction with variant selectors but low ATC conversion — the dropdown format added friction to a decision that should be immediate. Chips surface all options without an extra click.
Implementation: ~35 hrs · Primary KPIs: ATC rate, CVR

What This Engagement Demonstrates

  • A diagnostic framework is faster than it looks. 50 hours of structured diagnostic work prevents months of poorly-aimed testing. The alternative — guessing at hypotheses from week one — typically produces lower-confidence tests with weaker signals and no compounding rationale.
  • The test roadmap is only as good as the diagnostic behind it. Each of the three tests above has a specific data trail: which workstream surfaced it, which metric it's addressing, and what behavioral signal justified it. That traceability is what makes the difference between a test program and a test calendar.
  • Review mining and VoC surveys are the most underused diagnostic tools in CRO. Quantitative data tells you where users drop off. Qualitative data tells you why. A test roadmap built only on analytics will optimize the wrong things with high statistical confidence.
